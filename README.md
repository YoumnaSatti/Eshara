##  Project Idea
["Eshara" is an innovative mobile application (Android & iOS) designed to empower the newly deaf, their families, and the wider community to master Egyptian Sign Language (ESL) through a modern, user-centered experience. It addresses the critical lack of specialized local resources by offering interactive lessons, smart flashcards for rapid vocabulary retention, and a cutting-edge AI Multimodal Chat that provides real-time translation between signs, text, and voice via camera integration. By offering a cost-effective and accessible alternative to traditional training, the project will be presented as a vital daily tool that breaks communication barriers and transforms sign language into an inclusive, everyday skill for everyone.]

##  1. Research & Analysis
[Conducted qualitative research through interviews with sign language learners and those who struggled due to the lack of specialized resources, to understand the unique challenges of learning Egyptian Sign Language (ESL). Created empathy maps and user personas to pinpoint motivations and frustrations, while analyzing competitors to identify gaps in localized Egyptian content.]

---

##  2. Problem Definition
[Aspiring learners of sign language in Egypt face fundamental barriers to effective communication. Current applications lack specialization in Egyptian Sign Language (ESL), often providing superficial content limited to alphabets rather than comprehensive curricula covering daily phrases and conversations. Furthermore, learners suffer from a 'practice gap'; without interactive environments or training partners, vocabulary is quickly forgotten, leading to frustration. This severe lack of localized, tech-accessible resources makes traditional training costly and inaccessible, leaving the newly deaf and their families in a state of communication isolation.]
